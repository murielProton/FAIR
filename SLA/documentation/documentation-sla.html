Service Level Agreement

The Service Level Agreement is a legal agreement we have with our clients. When a ticket comes in, it is timed according to it's type, it's priority and wether or not it has a bypass.

There are two records types : Bug and User Story.

Bug

Bugs are timed from the moment we receive them to the moment we respond to them. They are also time from the moment we start to correct them to the moment they are corrected.

Length of time to respond to a Bug

The length of time calculated from the moment we receive a ticket to the moment we respond to it, concerns two fields on agf__ADM_Work__c : Duration_In_MS_To_Respond_To_A_Bug__c and Duration_In_String_To_Respond_To_A_Bug__c.

Length of time to correct a Bug

The length of time calculated from the moment we statuate a ticket to 'Developpement in Progress', to the moment the status is set to 'Validated To Be Deployed'. The time available to find a correction depends on if we have a bypass or not. The priority influence also greatly the timing.

The bypass is the checkbox : Bypass_For_Corrective_Maintenance_On_Bug__c.

Uer Story

User Stories are timed from the moment we receive them to the moment we respond to them. And then an there it stops. It concerns only the durations fields : Duration_MS_Status_New__c and Duration_MS_Status_Workload_DONE__c. At the exact moment the status is set to 'OK For DEV' the timer stops.
